Echoes of Conflict
Tracing War Themes in a Century of State of the Union Addresses
The State of the Union address is a yearly speech delivered by the President of the United States to Congress. It outlines the nation's condition and sets forth the legislative agenda. "Echoes of Conflict," maps out the prevalence of war rhetoric in the State of the Union addresses over the last century. Through a radial bar chart, each year is represented by a bar, with the length indicating what proportion of the speech was dedicated to discussing war. The design is straightforward: longer bars signal more extensive talk of war, and shorter bars, less so.
This data-driven approach provides a revealing look at how war has ebbed and flowed as a subject in the annual addresses to Congress, reflecting the nation's entanglement with international and domestic conflicts over time.
